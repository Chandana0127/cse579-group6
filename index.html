Core finding

Hallucination caught — Vector-Only mode
Vector-Only retrieved MSFT revenue chunks and attributed them to AAPL. Claimed $281,724M and $245,122M as Apple figures. M3 checker flagged both with 8.2% and 17.0% deviation from KG ground truth.
KG-grounded modes: 0 hallucinations
KG-Only and Hybrid both produced 0% hallucination rate across all 6 evaluation queries. KG entity grounding anchors retrieval to the correct company ticker.
F1 score alone is insufficient
Vector-Only scores highest Token F1 (0.165) but is the mode that hallucinated. This demonstrates why consistency checking is a necessary addition beyond standard RAG evaluation metrics.

What happened
Vector-Only FAISS retrieved Microsoft revenue chunks and placed them in Apple context. The LLM generated confident-sounding figures. The checker cross-referenced each against the AAPL KG entity store and flagged both MSFT figures as contradicting Apple ground truth.
Why this matters
Token F1 scoring would not have caught this — the numbers are plausible-sounding financial figures and would produce partial F1 overlap. Only KG-grounded consistency checking can detect cross-company attribution errors.
